Why Stride exists

The specialist who can close the planning gap is rare.

Mining operations spend hundreds of millions on planned component replacements. Most of those replacements run on fleet-average benchmarks — "replace the engine at twelve thousand hours" — applied to units that run fourteen-hour shifts in hard-rock conditions. The result is either premature replacement (waste) or unplanned failure (worse). The specialist who can close that gap by working from real utilisation, real component history, and real condition data is rare, expensive, and not scalable as a person. Stride is the structural answer: one specialist serving multiple clients, delivering the same depth of analysis, because the software does the work that does not require a human.

Four strategic pillars

Every product decision serves one of these.

Stride is built on four pillars. They guide what the service includes, what the platform builds, and what is deliberately left out.

Pillar 01

Specialise deeply.

Long-term component life planning, reliability analysis, fleet sustainability. Not day-to-day dispatch, not generalist asset management. Depth of specialisation is the defence in a market where ERP vendors keep widening their feature sets.

Pillar 02

Productise the service.

Defined scope. Fixed monthly fee. Repeatable delivery. The platform makes consistent quality possible across multiple clients with finite advisor time. Without productisation, scaling means working harder. With it, scaling means running the same process more times.

Pillar 03

Build annuity income.

Monthly retainer revenue replaces the project-based income model. The shift is structural — from a consulting practice where the asset is one person's time to a service business where the asset is the relationship, the process, and the tool.

Pillar 04

Cross-client data moat.

Every engagement adds anonymised component-life data to a growing dataset, keyed by deterministic component IDs. At five clients, the dataset is something nobody else has. That dataset makes the service permanently defensible — and eventually, in the form of Signal, separately licensable.

What a Stride engagement delivers.

The scope is defined and consistent. Each item below is a recurring deliverable, not an optional add-on. The advisor brings specialist interpretation; Lens makes the work consistent across one client or five.

  • 01
    Rolling two-year PCR schedule Maintained continuously. Date forecasting from current SMU, utilisation profiles, and component history. Auto-clustered into project packages where components fall in the same window.
  • 02
    Monthly fleet-health review One session per month with the client. Built around the data in Lens — not reconstructed from spreadsheets the night before. Output: a written summary, the updated schedule, the decisions taken.
  • 03
    Capital forecasting Multi-cycle budget projection by fleet, year, and site. Updated each month against the latest schedule. The view a finance team needs to budget component replacements three to five years out.
  • 04
    Shutdown sequencing Optimised project sequencing across the fleet to minimise production impact and resource conflicts. Each shutdown carries a structured work package — pre-event, day-of, closeout.
  • 05
    ERP and condition-monitoring integration Periodic ingestion of SAP exports (IW39, SMU, materials master) and condition-monitoring reports through the Intake companion tool. Weekly SMU, monthly IW39, as-needed ConMon.
  • 06
    Specialist advisory time The interpretation layer. The advisor does the work that requires specialist knowledge — what a result means, what to recommend, how to phrase it for the client. The platform handles the rest.
Who it's for

Three people read the same outputs differently.

A Stride engagement serves the same fleet, but the data lands on different desks for different reasons. The platform is built so each persona can find what they need without consulting another system.

Fleet Manager / Owner

The thirty-thousand-foot view.

Needs to know overall fleet health, top risks, upcoming major cost events. Stride delivers the monthly summary; the platform answers the in-month questions without a follow-up email.

Planner / Scheduler

Always three to twelve months ahead.

Needs to know upcoming PCRs, resource requirements, cost forecast, schedule conflicts. Cadence is the daily working surface; the rolling plan stays current rather than ageing on a shared drive.

Maintenance Superintendent

Lives in the present.

Needs to know units in workshop, active work orders, condition-monitoring alerts, overdue items. Pulse and Vitals are the surfaces; Deploy is where in-flight projects sit.

How engagement works

Three phases, then a monthly rhythm.

Onboarding is light. Most of the value lands inside the first ninety days, and the monthly cadence keeps it current.

Phase 01

Intake

Client data exports run through the Intake companion tool. Fleet register mapped, components classified, history loaded. Two hours per fleet for an experienced operation; longer for tier-one engagements with no CMMS history.

Phase 02

Lens setup

Utilisation profiles configured, benchmarks applied, the rolling schedule generated for the first time. Walkthrough session with the client to validate the model and resolve any gaps in the source data.

Phase 03

Monthly cadence

Monthly review session. Schedule updated against the latest data. Decisions captured in the file. Client receives the written summary, the updated PCR plan, and the capital projection for the rolling horizon.
