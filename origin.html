Origin · Why NumaCore exists

Built by the practice that uses it.

NumaCore is not a commercial product looking for a market. It started as a working tool inside an advisory practice that needed planning depth no off-the-shelf system could deliver.

The frustration was specific. Mining operations run highly capable ERP systems — SAP, Pronto, JD Edwards — that hold enormous amounts of equipment and maintenance history. But the interfaces are built for accountants, system administrators, and procurement; not for the maintenance manager trying to decide whether to pull an engine before the next quarter's production push, or risk running it to failure.

So the planning work happens elsewhere. Excel pivots. Stitched-together Power BI reports. Info packs assembled by whoever has been there longest. Schedules entered into one system, decisions made in another, and the gap between them filled by tribal knowledge in the heads of experienced planners — people who eventually leave. The cost of getting this wrong is enormous. A premature replacement costs tens of thousands in unnecessary parts and labour. A late one costs production, safety, and emergency repair bills that dwarf the planned cost.

Birchwood Advisory built NumaCore in-house because no commercial tool handled long-range component planning at the depth a multi-fleet advisory practice required. The platform is not licensed to clients. It is not a SaaS product. It is the delivery engine behind every Birchwood long-range planning engagement — the reason the rolling plan stays current month after month, rather than ageing on a shared drive. The advisory practice is at birchwood-advisory.com.

The principal is Willie. Thirty years in mobile-equipment maintenance across mining, civil, and heavy industry — not a high-flying business consultant with a pedigree MBA, but a practitioner. Years in the planning room, on site, managing schedules, wrestling SAP, making mistakes, learning. The platform is shaped by that experience. The decisions about what NumaCore does and does not do come from the daily struggle, because it was the daily struggle.

Not licensed. Not a SaaS. The reason long-range plans built here stay current.
